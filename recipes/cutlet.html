Cutlet

Description of the Recipe

A Cutlet is a dish made from minced meat or vegetables, shaped into a patty, coated in breadcrumbs, and fried or baked. It offers a crispy exterior and is commonly prepared with meats like chicken, pork, or beef, as well as vegetarian options.Cutlets are versatile, suitable for standalone meals, sandwiches, or burgers, and come in various regional variations.

Ingredients Required

  • Ground Meat or Vegetables
  • Bread Crumbs
  • Egg
  • Onion
  • Garlic and Ginger
  • Herbs and Spices
  • Flour
  • Oil
  • Garnishing ingredients

Procedure Involved

  1. Prepare Ingredients
    • If using ground meat, ensure it's thawed if frozen. If using vegetables, cook and mash or grate them.
    • Finely chop onions, mince garlic, and grate ginger.
    • Gather herbs and spices, bread crumbs, egg, flour, and oil.

  2. Cook Onions, Garlic, and Ginger
    • Heat a small amount of oil in a pan over medium heat.
    • Sauté chopped onions until translucent.
    • Add minced garlic and grated ginger, sauté until fragrant. Allow it to cool.

  3. Mix Ingredients
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat or mashed vegetables, sautéed onions, garlic, and ginger.
    • Add herbs and spices (parsley, coriander, cumin, paprika, salt, pepper) according to your taste.

  4. Add Breadcrumbs and Egg
    • Incorporate bread crumbs into the mixture. The crumbs act as a binder.
    • Crack an egg into the mixture, which helps hold the ingredients together. Mix thoroughly.

  5. Shape into Cutlets
    • Take portions of the mixture and shape them into flat, round patties or cutlets.

  6. Coat with Flour, Dip in Egg, and Roll in Breadcrumbs
    • Dust each cutlet with flour to help the egg stick.
    • Dip each cutlet into beaten egg.
    • Roll the cutlet in breadcrumbs until evenly coated.

  7. Heat Oil for Frying
    • In a pan, heat enough oil for shallow frying over medium-high heat.

  8. Fry the Cutlets
    • Carefully place the coated cutlets in the hot oil.
    • Fry each side until golden brown and cooked through. This usually takes 3-4 minutes per side.

  9. Drain and Serve
    • Once cooked, place the cutlets on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
    • Serve hot with your favorite sauce or chutney.
